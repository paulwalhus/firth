Dedicated Fan Sites

Fan Sites & Tributes

These sites are run by fans, for fans — each with its own character, focus and history. Some have been going for years; others are newer arrivals. All are worth a visit.

Fan Site • Archive
firth.com
firth.com
The original and longest-running Colin Firth fan site, first launched in the late 1990s. It ran continuously for nearly seventeen years before going dark in 2015. The Wayback Machine preserved its vast archive — over 500 pages, 1,900 images, interviews, reviews and multimedia going back to 1984 — and this restored edition brings it back online in 2026. The closest thing Colin Firth has to an official fan archive on the web.

Reference & Research

Databases & Encyclopaedias

The essential reference stops for credits, ratings, biographical data and critical consensus.

Database • Essential
IMDb — Colin Firth
imdb.com/name/nm0000147/
The Internet Movie Database is the definitive starting point for any actor’s credits. Colin’s page covers his complete filmography from Crown Court (1984) to the present, with full cast and crew listings, user ratings, trivia, quotes and biography. The external sites directory on his IMDb page also links to fan resources across the web.

Database • Ratings
Rotten Tomatoes — Colin Firth
rottentomatoes.com/celebrity/colin_firth
Rotten Tomatoes aggregates critical and audience scores for every Colin Firth film. Indispensable for seeing at a glance how critics received each project — from the 97% for The King’s Speech to the more mixed receptions for some of his lighter fare. Also lists his highest-rated work by Tomatometer.

Note • Social Media
Colin Firth is not on social media
No official accounts on any platform
Colin Firth does not have a verified presence on Instagram, X / Twitter, Facebook or any other social media platform. All accounts using his name or likeness are fan-run. He has spoken about his deliberate choice to remain off social media in several interviews, describing it as a personal preference for privacy and direct human interaction over curated public personas.

About ColinFirth.com

This Site

ColinFirth.com was first launched in the late 1990s and ran for nearly seventeen years as one of the internet’s most comprehensive actor fan sites. At its peak it contained over 500 HTML pages, 1,900 images, hundreds of interviews, reviews, multimedia files and original editorial content covering every aspect of Colin’s career from 1984 onward.

The site went offline in early 2015. The Wayback Machine preserved its archive, and this restored version — rebuilt and brought up to date in 2026 — attempts to honour the spirit and depth of the original while bringing the design and content into the present. The goal is a site worthy of one of the finest actors of his generation.

This is a fan site and is not affiliated with or endorsed by Colin Firth, his management, or any of his production or representation partners. All content is produced by fans for fans.
