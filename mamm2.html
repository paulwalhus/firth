The Story

Synopsis

A dual-timeline sequel that unfolds simultaneously in 1979 and the present day. In the past, a young Donna (Lily James) arrives on the Greek island of Kalokairi and falls into the three brief romances that will result in Sophie’s birth. In the present, Sophie (Amanda Seyfried) prepares to reopen and rededicate her late mother’s beloved hotel — Donna having died between films.

Colin returns as Harry Bright, one of the three possible fathers, now settled into a domestic life sketched with warm comedy. The present-day sequences see Harry, Sam (Pierce Brosnan) and Bill (Stellan Skarsgård) making their way to the island for the reopening — a reunion threaded through with grief for Donna.

The film’s most celebrated sequence involves Harry and Bill dancing exuberantly together on the bow of a boat to “Dancing Queen” — a Titanic-style pose that became an instant fan favourite. Cher appears memorably in the third act as Sophie’s glamorous, long-absent grandmother Ruby Sheridan.

Production

Notes

  • Released July 20, 2018. Worldwide gross: $395 million.
  • Directed by Ol Parker, who co-wrote the screenplay for the original 2008 film. Producer Judy Craymer returned for both.
  • Filmed partly on Vis Island, Croatia — the same location used for the original.
  • Richard Curtis cameos as young Harry’s uncle. Young Harry is played by Hugh Skinner.
  • The present-day storyline deals explicitly with Donna’s death; Meryl Streep appears mainly in flashback and dream sequences.
  • Many critics considered it a step above the original, particularly praising the 1979 sequences and Lily James’s performance as young Donna.
